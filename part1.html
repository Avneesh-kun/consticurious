Part I:
The Union and Its Territory

Article 1 Article 2 Article 3 Article 4

Article 1

Name and Territory of the Union

  • India, that is Bharat, shall be a Union of States.
  • The territory of India includes:
    1. Territories of the States,
    2. Union Territories,
    3. Any territories that may be acquired in the future.

Article 2

Admission or Establishment of New States

  • Parliament has the power to admit new states into the Union of India.
  • Parliament can establish new states as needed.

Article 3

Formation of New States and Alteration of Boundaries

  • Parliament can:
    1. Form a new state by separating territory from an existing state.
    2. Merge two or more states or parts of states.
    3. Alter the boundary or name of any state.
  • Such changes require the approval of Parliament, and the opinion of the affected state's legislature must be sought (though their consent is not mandatory).

Article 4

Laws Related to Article 2 and 3

  • Laws made under Articles 2 and 3 for forming new states or altering boundaries are not considered constitutional amendments, meaning they can be passed by a simple majority in Parliament.

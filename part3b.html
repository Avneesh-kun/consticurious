Right to Equality

Article 14 Article 15 Article 16 Article 17 Article 18

Article 14

Equality Before the Law

  • All individuals are equal before the law and must be treated equally.

Article 15

Prohibition of Discrimination

  • The State cannot discriminate against anyone based on religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.
  • Exceptions include affirmative action for socially and educationally backward classes, Scheduled Castes (SCs), and Scheduled Tribes (STs).

Article 16

Equality in Public Employment

  • Everyone has an equal right to apply for government jobs, regardless of religion, caste, or gender.
  • Exceptions exist for reservations for disadvantaged groups.

Article 17

Abolition of Untouchability

  • Untouchability is abolished and punishable by law.

Article 18

Abolition of Titles

  • Titles like "Raja" or "Maharaja" are prohibited, except for academic or military distinctions.

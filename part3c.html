Article 19

Protection of Six Freedoms

  • Citizens have the right to:
    1. Freedom of speech and expression.
    2. Assemble peacefully without arms.
    3. Form associations or unions.
    4. Move freely across India.
    5. Reside and settle in any part of India.
    6. Practice any profession or business.
  • These freedoms are subject to reasonable restrictions (e.g., for public order).

Article 20

Protection in Respect of Convictions

  • No person can be:
    1. Punished under laws that were not in force at the time of the offense (no retrospective punishment).
    2. Tried for the same offense twice (no double jeopardy).
    3. Forced to incriminate themselves.

Article 21

Protection of Life and Personal Liberty

  • No one can be deprived of life or liberty except according to a legal process.

Article 21A

Right to Education

  • Children aged 6-14 have the right to free and compulsory education.

Article 22

Protection Against Arrest and Detention

  • Provides safeguards against arbitrary arrest and detention, especially preventive detention (limited to specific circumstances).

Article 5

Citizenship at the Commencement of the Constitution

  • Any person who had their domicile (permanent residence) in India and:
    1. Was born in India,
    2. Or had either parent born in India,
    3. Or had been an ordinary resident in India for at least 5 years before the Constitution came into force, automatically became a citizen of India.

Article 6

Rights of People Who Migrated from Pakistan

  • People who migrated from Pakistan to India before July 19, 1948, became Indian citizens if :
    1. They resided in India since their migration.
  • People who migrated after this date could still become citizens if :
    1. They registered as citizens of India after living in India for at least 6 months.

Article 7

Rights of People Who Migrated to Pakistan and Returned

  • If a person migrated to Pakistan after March 1, 1947, but later returned to India, they could become an Indian citizen if they had permission from Indian authorities to resettle.

Article 8

Citizenship for Indians Living Abroad

  • A person born in India or with Indian parentage, living outside India, could register as an Indian citizen at the Indian embassy or consulate in their country.

Article 9

Loss of Citizenship by Acquiring Foreign Citizenship

  • A person who voluntarily acquired the citizenship of another country after January 26, 1950, automatically lost their Indian citizenship.

Article 10

Continuance of Citizenship

  • Any person who is a citizen of India under the Constitution continues to be a citizen unless disqualified by a law made by Parliament.
